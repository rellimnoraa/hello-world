Red Deck Wins

This deck is pretty straight forward: Keep smashing!

This is my bread and butter from when I began playing paper magic.

I've always been an RDW guy, and, on the occasion I can get out to play paper, I usually try to scrounge up some form of the deck to bring with me.

When Consulate Dreadnought was spoiled before Aether Revolt release, I knew immediately it was going to find a home in here.

Some (or most) players would say it's a terrible card, but I play the cards I find interesting, and, if they use a removal spell on the Dreadnought,well, I have more guys to throw at 'em!"

Mad Mill

Mad Mill is a deck I discovered from LegenVD's Magic Duels YouTube channel.

This deck relies on you burning through your library so that you can dump your opponent's library into their graveyard, milling them out.

The center piece to this masterpiece, of course, is Sphynx's Tutelage. Combining this card with the artment of draw spells and abilities allows allows you mill your opponent fairly quickly.

It's not uncommon, if you play your cards right, to mill 20+ cards in a single turn. I know I've saved the game more than a few times by managing a win this way.

The "Mad" in "Mad Mill" comes from a few madness spells. Many spells and abilities in the deck has the pilot discarding cards as a resource.

In Amonkhet's upcoming release, I am looking forward to adding Drake Haven to take advantage of the discard affects, as well as the potential to add cycling for more card draw effects.
